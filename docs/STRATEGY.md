# Tips & strategy

## General

1. **Engineers win games**, not tanks. Every mission ends because someone
   captured something. Build at least 2 engineers turn 1–2 and push them
   toward enemy HQ / objective tiles immediately.
2. **The 3-turn capture rule** means a single engineer left undefended will
   die. Always escort with one combat unit per engineer once you're past
   the early game.
3. **Hunker (`H`) before the enemy turn** if you're holding ground. +2 DEF
   converts roughly 30% of incoming damage into nothing, and the unit still
   counter-attacks.
4. **Don't kamikaze with `attack mode`.** Hover the target — if `<-`
   counter > your remaining HP, the suicide-penalty AI logic should also
   warn you. Retreat (`R`) or hunker instead.

## Combined arms

The damage matrix forces rock-paper-scissors:

- **AA shreds air.** If the enemy is flying anything, put an AA unit near
  your front line. AA is weak against ground, so screen it with infantry.
- **Jets shred ground but get murdered by AA.** Use jets to flank around
  enemy AA, not into it.
- **Artillery + recon = murder.** Recon spots from cover, artillery hits
  from out of counter range. Spend turn 1 unlocking T2 if you can afford it
  for indirect fire.
- **Snipers vs vehicles is suicide.** Snipers are infantry murderers only.

## Map awareness

- **Forests** give +2 DEF for foot units. Park infantry there to bait.
- **Cities** are +3 DEF AND tick credits. Capturing a city pays for itself
  in 5–8 turns.
- **Mountains** block line-of-sight as well as movement. Use them to hide
  artillery or spring stealth ambushes.
- **Bridges** are choke points. Whoever holds the bridge controls the side.
  Hunker a tank on a bridge — it's almost unkillable.

## Difficulty notes

- **Normal**: the AI plays it straight. Engineers, builds, advances. You
  should win comfortably with combined arms.
- **Hard**: AI gets +400cr/turn 1 and runs an "aggressive" personality
  (more weight on attacks, less on retreat). Expect early pressure.
- **Insane**: +900cr/turn 1 and the "predator" personality — the AI almost
  never retreats, kills aggressively, and rushes engineers. You'll need
  perfect economy and good chokepoint defence to survive.

## Faction-specific

### Playing NATO
- Tech rush. T2 unlocks at 1000cr — pay it on turn 2 if possible.
- One jet wins air superiority for the whole game unless they have AA.
- Engineers are slow (move 3) but tougher (DEF 1) — escort always.

### Playing BRICS
- You will always have more units than you "should." Spend, don't hoard.
- Iskander-M (T3) reaches across most maps. Save 2500cr for tier 3 once
  your front is stable.
- Swarm drones are flying skirmishers — fast, cheap, harass artillery.

### Playing Guerilla
- Stealth means the AI walks into your scouts blind. Position scouts on
  enemy approach routes early.
- Kamikaze drones are one-shot — use them to assassinate high-value targets
  (artillery, generals, anything wounded).
- No T3 at all. Don't waste credits trying to upgrade past T2.

## End-turn reminder

The HUD shows `N units can act` in the top-right. If you press end-turn
with units still able to act, a modal asks "are you sure?" — say no and use
Tab to cycle through them.
